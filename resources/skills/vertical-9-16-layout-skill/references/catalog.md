# Vertical 9:16 Layout Catalog

This catalog describes 900x1600 vertical story structures. It is structural only: style controls color, type mood, decoration, shadows, and texture.

## Zone Skeletons

### `top-middle-bottom`

- **Use for**: most explainers, summaries, and data takeaways.
- **Zone sketch**: top hook/title, middle value/proof, bottom takeaway/support.
- **Balance rule**: the middle must carry the page; top and bottom should frame it.
- **Failure sign**: title and small cards sit near the top while the middle/lower screen is passive background.

### `hero-center`

- **Use for**: quote, big claim, hero metric, or single insight.
- **Zone sketch**: compact context at top, large hero object around visual center, small proof/takeaway below.
- **Balance rule**: low density is valid when the hero is large and memorable.
- **Failure sign**: support elements compete with the hero.

### `lane-stack`

- **Use for**: steps, roadmap, ranked list, or phased explanation.
- **Zone sketch**: hook/title, vertical lanes or rows through the middle, short summary at bottom.
- **Balance rule**: rows must have consistent rhythm and readable spacing.
- **Failure sign**: the sequence becomes many uneven paragraph cards.

## Patterns

### `hook-value-takeaway`

- **Input shape**: one claim plus 2-4 support facts.
- **Structure recipe**: top hook/title, middle proof/value zone, bottom takeaway band.
- **Budget rule**: hook 15-25%, middle 45-60%, bottom 15-25%.
- **Failure signs**: no bottom memory point; middle is too small to support the hook.

### `vertical-step-story`

- **Input shape**: 3-5 ordered steps, phases, or methods.
- **Structure recipe**: numbered vertical sequence, timeline lane, or staircase; each step has a short label and 1-2 lines.
- **Budget rule**: distribute steps through the middle height instead of clustering them.
- **Failure signs**: step text becomes paragraphs; connectors overlap text; more than six steps crowd the screen.

### `stacked-comparison`

- **Input shape**: before/after, problem/solution, old/new, option A/B.
- **Structure recipe**: two stacked zones or aligned comparison rows with matching fields.
- **Budget rule**: each side gets comparable height and field count.
- **Failure signs**: one side has far more fields; contrast is not visible until reading every sentence.

### `data-takeaway`

- **Input shape**: one chart, trend, rank list, or hero metric with interpretation.
- **Structure recipe**: hook/title, one dominant data object, visible interpretation sentence, optional 1-2 support chips.
- **Budget rule**: reserve chart/list height first; interpretation stays near the data.
- **Failure signs**: chart is a tiny appendix; chart has many labels with no readable interpretation.

### `ranked-list`

- **Input shape**: 4-7 priorities, findings, recommendations, or ordered items.
- **Structure recipe**: full-width rows with number, label, and concise detail.
- **Budget rule**: rows share stable heights and fill the middle zone with rhythm.
- **Failure signs**: rows become separate floating cards; details wrap into long paragraphs.

### `hero-claim`

- **Input shape**: one quote, thesis, lesson, or bold conclusion.
- **Structure recipe**: large claim around visual center, optional context above/below, 1-2 proof chips.
- **Budget rule**: claim owns the page; support stays subordinate.
- **Failure signs**: many equal cards dilute the claim; hero text is too small.
