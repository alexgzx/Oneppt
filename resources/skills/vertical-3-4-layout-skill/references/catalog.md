# Vertical 3:4 Layout Catalog

This catalog describes 1200x1600 vertical poster-card structures. It is structural only: style controls color, type mood, decoration, shadows, and texture.

## Zone Skeletons

### `poster-anchor`

- **Use for**: poster claims, hero metrics, and concept cards.
- **Zone sketch**: strong top/upper anchor, main proof/value zone, bottom synthesis/source.
- **Balance rule**: the anchor must dominate without crowding the proof zone.
- **Failure sign**: many equal cards replace the poster anchor.

### `section-stack`

- **Use for**: checklists, process pages, evidence groups.
- **Zone sketch**: title/claim, 2-4 grouped vertical sections, short bottom memory point.
- **Balance rule**: sections should be grouped by meaning and sized by importance.
- **Failure sign**: sections are only a long list of cards.

### `wide-pocket`

- **Use for**: short parallel comparisons or evidence pairs inside one section.
- **Zone sketch**: full-width context, compact two-column pocket, full-width synthesis.
- **Balance rule**: the two-column pocket is a local device, not the whole layout.
- **Failure sign**: both columns become paragraph-heavy.

## Patterns

### `poster-hero-proof`

- **Input shape**: one strong claim with 2-4 proof points.
- **Structure recipe**: large claim, main proof zone, compact evidence band or bottom conclusion.
- **Budget rule**: hero zone is visibly larger than support.
- **Failure signs**: viewer cannot identify the anchor; proof points become equal heroes.

### `hero-metric-explainer`

- **Input shape**: one key number or result plus context.
- **Structure recipe**: hero metric, short interpretation, 1-3 support rows/chips.
- **Budget rule**: metric owns a large vertical zone; support stays compact.
- **Failure signs**: multiple numbers compete; interpretation is detached from the metric.

### `vertical-process`

- **Input shape**: 3-6 steps, phases, or layers.
- **Structure recipe**: vertical timeline, staircase, or stacked bands.
- **Budget rule**: distribute steps through the middle/lower canvas.
- **Failure signs**: steps become paragraphs; lower canvas is unused.

### `comparison-rows`

- **Input shape**: two options/states compared across shared dimensions.
- **Structure recipe**: aligned rows with labels, or two stacked zones with matching fields.
- **Budget rule**: field count stays consistent and rows remain short.
- **Failure signs**: unequal field counts; contrast hidden in prose.

### `evidence-band-stack`

- **Input shape**: 3-5 facts supporting one conclusion.
- **Structure recipe**: conclusion block plus grouped evidence bands, chips, or rows.
- **Budget rule**: facts are grouped by meaning, not expanded into full cards by default.
- **Failure signs**: evidence reads as scattered facts; too many equal blocks.

### `two-column-pocket`

- **Input shape**: one section with parallel short items.
- **Structure recipe**: one vertical section contains a compact two-column subgrid; surrounding sections stay full-width.
- **Budget rule**: each item is short and aligned.
- **Failure signs**: the pocket becomes the whole page; text is too narrow.

### `data-card`

- **Input shape**: one chart, rank list, hero metric, or compact table.
- **Structure recipe**: title/claim, main data object, interpretation sentence, optional 1-2 support chips.
- **Budget rule**: data object owns the middle zone and interpretation stays nearby.
- **Failure signs**: data is hard to read; interpretation is missing or too far away.
